About

The "Urban Heat Island" phenomenon refers to the atmospheric condition where temperatures in urban areas are higher than those in the surrounding regions. This phenomenon is often associated with the human-made structures and activities in urban environments. Several factors contribute to the Urban Heat Island effect

  • Built Infrastructure
  • Reduced Vegetation
  • Human Activities

The Urban Heat Island effect can have several consequences, including elevated temperatures, increased energy consumption for cooling, and potential health risks. Mitigation strategies may involve incorporating green spaces, implementing cool roofing technologies, and urban planning to minimize heat retention.
